What is TachoPrint?

To comply with DVSA legislation all drivers must be able to produce a record of all their work and rest time for a period of at least 28 days. TachoPrint enables you to record all your time that is not recorded on your tacho card, be it rest, breaks, work, holiday, or sickness by means of using your smartphone combined with our app to enable you to provide a printout on tacho printout paper as per DVSA guidance using your mini thermal printer.

TachoPrint in Action

For this demonstration and assessment our imaginary driver Mr Peter Piper drove our heavy goods vehicle for the whole of January and Peter recorded all his work, breaks and rest on his tachograph inline with DVSA regulations.

On Friday 29 th January at the end of his shift at 18:00 hrs Peter was informed that there were not many deliveries for Monday February 1 st and he would therefore be using our 3.5-ton vehicle which did not have a tachograph to do these deliveries. On Tuesday 2 nd February there again were few deliveries and again Peter would be using the van to do these deliveries. On Wednesday 3 rd Peter reverted to using his HGV and recorded all his work etc on his tachograph for Wednesday, Thursday, and Friday morning from 08:00 until 12:00 when Peter then removed his card and took a 1hr break before commencing work in the warehouse on Friday afternoon from 13:00 hrs until his shift ended at 18:00 hrs.

Peter at this point decided that although he was confident in doing manual entries on a tachograph for a single entry, he was not confident enough to do multiple manual entries and therefore decided to do entries on tacho printout paper to comply with EC regulation 561/2006 . To achieve his aim of doing what is required with the least effort and suitably able to satisfy DVSA in respect of keeping a full set of records of his activities, Peter decided to use our TachoPrint system to achieve the desired results.

DVSA Compliance

Drivers' hours: recording of other work Article 6(5) of retained Regulation (EC) 561/2006) requires manual records to be kept of all work (including out of scope driving and any other work) and periods of availability, using either the manual inputs on a digital/smart tachograph or by making a manual record on a record sheet (from analogue tachographs) or on printout paper (from digital tachographs)

Along with other parts of the regulations in place before August 2020, the changes mean that all drivers (including occasional drivers) must keep a full set of records of their activities for the current day and the previous 28 days.
